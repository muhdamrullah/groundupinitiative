Ground-Up Initiative

Ground-Up Initiative (GUI) is founded to nurture resilient, grounded stewards of our society by empowering them to discover their sense of purpose.

Vision

This is a vision of GUI’s founder Mr Tay Lai Hock, who is deeply inspired by this Chinese idiom.

Lai Hock believes that ownership and responsibility towards society comes with understanding our inherent symbiotic connection to our environment. The more we are nurtured spiritually and psychologically by it, the more we will understand how important it is conserve this inspiring life-giving force.

Space

Kampung Kampus - The School of Life

GUI has created Kampung Kampus, which is being consciously architected on an ambitious 26,000 sqm space at Khatib, in a land-scarce Singapore.

It is not just a physical space – witnessing people’s lives changed, their meaning redefined, leaders fostered, communities brought together, and much more, through a range of diverse initiatives.

It is a space containing amazing stories of people's evolution:

“GUI has made me a better son, husband, citizen to the country and a person living on this earth.”
– Cai Bingyu, 32, Architect

Initiatives

A range of diverse social enterprises are carefully designed to cultivate a multitude of strengths for a wide spectrum of individuals and groups, forming a robust, nurturing eco-system of GUI within the Kampung Kampus:

  • Wow Kampung

    a social enterprise for experiential environmental education and character development for students and teachers.

  • Touchwood

    encourages practical craft skills such as carpentry.

  • Farmily

    which encourages a healthy lifestyle and extra income for the elderly through urban farming.

  • GUI Labs for Development (GUILD)

    a social enterprise for experiential environmental education and character development for students and teachers.

  • Ground-Up Troopers (GUTS)

    a programme to nurture grounded leaders based on “Thinker~Warrior~Farmer” philosophy to initiate more social and environmental changes in Singapore. This batch of GUTS training has commenced in Jan 2016.
